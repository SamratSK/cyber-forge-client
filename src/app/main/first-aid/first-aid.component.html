First Aid During Natural Disasters

Essential first aid measures to take during various natural disasters such as floods, earthquakes, tsunamis, and more.

Floods

  • Move to Higher Ground: Immediately move to higher ground to avoid floodwaters.
  • Avoid Floodwaters: Do not walk or drive through moving water. Just 6 inches of moving water can knock you down.
  • Stay Dry: Keep yourself dry to prevent hypothermia.
  • Use Clean Water: Drink only clean, boiled, or bottled water to avoid waterborne diseases.
  • Wound Care: Clean wounds with clean water and apply an antiseptic to prevent infection. Cover with a clean bandage.
  • CPR: Be prepared to perform CPR if someone is unconscious or not breathing due to drowning.

Earthquakes

  • Drop, Cover, and Hold On: Protect yourself during the shaking by getting under sturdy furniture and holding on until the shaking stops.
  • Check for Injuries: Once safe, check yourself and others for injuries. Provide basic first aid.
  • Stop Bleeding: Apply pressure to stop any bleeding with a clean cloth or bandage.
  • Splint Fractures: If someone has a fracture, immobilize the affected limb with a splint.
  • Treat for Shock: Keep the injured person lying down and warm until help arrives.
  • Clear Airways: If someone is unconscious, ensure their airway is clear and they are breathing.

Tsunamis

  • Evacuate Immediately: Move inland or to higher ground as soon as you hear a tsunami warning.
  • Check for Injuries: After the tsunami, check yourself and others for injuries and provide first aid.
  • Wound Care: Clean and cover wounds to prevent infection.
  • Stay Hydrated: Drink clean water to prevent dehydration, especially if stranded for an extended period.
  • CPR: Be prepared to administer CPR to those who may have drowned or are unconscious.
  • Monitor for Hypothermia: If someone is wet and cold, warm them gradually with blankets and dry clothing.

Hurricanes/Typhoons

  • Seek Shelter: Take refuge in a secure room away from windows and doors.
  • Treat Injuries: Address any injuries with basic first aid. Stop bleeding, treat fractures, and clean wounds.
  • Stay Dry and Warm: Protect yourself from the cold and wet conditions to prevent hypothermia.
  • Clean Water Supply: Ensure access to clean water to prevent dehydration and waterborne illnesses.
  • CPR: Be prepared to perform CPR on unconscious or non-breathing individuals.
  • Beware of Contaminated Water: Avoid floodwater, which can be contaminated with sewage or hazardous chemicals.

Landslides

  • Evacuate: Move away from the path of the landslide and to higher ground if possible.
  • Check for Injuries: Provide immediate first aid for any injuries, especially for those buried or trapped.
  • Stabilize Fractures: Immobilize broken limbs with a splint.
  • Treat Shock: Keep the injured warm and lying down to prevent shock.
  • Search for Others: If safe, help search for others who may be trapped or injured.
  • CPR: Be ready to perform CPR on unconscious or non-breathing individuals.

  • Treat Burns: Cool burns with running water, cover with a clean cloth, and do not apply creams or ointments.
  • Smoke Inhalation: Move to fresh air and monitor for respiratory distress. Provide rescue breathing or CPR if necessary.
  • Stay Hydrated: Drink plenty of water to prevent dehydration and heatstroke.
  • Protect Lungs: If trapped in smoke, use a wet cloth to cover your nose and mouth.
  • Monitor for Heat-Related Illnesses: Look out for symptoms of heat exhaustion or heatstroke, and treat by cooling the person and providing hydration.

Volcanic Eruptions

  • Evacuate: Move away from the eruption site and seek shelter indoors.
  • Protect Lungs and Skin: Use masks or cloths to cover your nose and mouth, and wear long clothing to protect your skin from ash.
  • Eye Protection: Wear goggles or glasses to protect your eyes from ash and debris.
  • First Aid for Burns: Cool any burns with clean, cool water and cover with a clean cloth.
  • Stay Hydrated: Drink plenty of water, especially in ash-filled environments.
  • Respiratory Distress: Monitor for and treat any signs of respiratory distress caused by inhaling volcanic ash.

Tornadoes

  • Shelter in Place: Seek refuge in a storm shelter, basement, or interior room away from windows.
  • Treat Wounds: Clean and cover wounds to prevent infection.
  • CPR: Provide CPR to anyone who is unconscious or not breathing.
  • Stabilize Fractures: Use splints to immobilize any broken bones.
  • Monitor for Shock: Keep the injured warm and calm until emergency services arrive.

General First Aid Supplies for Natural Disasters

  • First Aid Kit: Bandages, antiseptic wipes, gauze, adhesive tape, scissors, tweezers, etc.
  • Water and Food: Clean drinking water and non-perishable food.
  • Medications: Prescription medications, pain relievers, and any necessary medical supplies.
  • Blankets and Clothing: To prevent hypothermia and for comfort.
  • Flashlight and Batteries: For visibility in dark or unsafe areas.
  • Emergency Contacts: A list of emergency contacts and local emergency services.
  • CPR Mask: For safe administration of rescue breathing.
